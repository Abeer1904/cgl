About the Game

Poshan ki Potli is a card game designed as an easy, fun, and interactive method to help people, especially women, build comprehension around dietary diversity and food groups. It promotes nutrition literacy by connecting eating habits to locally available, inexpensive, nutritious foods and practical recipes.

The game adopts a scientific nutrition lens and uses language, verbs, and metaphors around nutrition. It draws on research by the National Institute of Nutrition. The deck includes regionally specific food items and recipes to help players learn how to build a nutritious plate.

It has been played across multiple states, and over 1,000 community members in Chhattisgarh and Uttarakhand have played the game in the field. It has also been used as part of UNICEF-supported initiatives and has travelled with the Active Citizenship Festival across campuses and practitioner groups.

Gameplay & Design

  • Format: Card game with multiple play modes (progressive learning).
  • Players: Flexible; community groups, families, SHGs, youth.
  • Core mechanic: Food-group comprehension through pairing, sorting, and recipe-based reasoning.
  • Learning curve: Six different ways to play, each building on the previous.

What It Explores

  • System: household food choices, nutrition literacy, and practical dietary diversity.
  • Variables: affordability, availability, habits, food-group knowledge, recipes, seasonal constraints.
  • Trade-offs: cost vs variety, convenience vs nutrition, tradition vs new practice.
  • Behavioural insight: people adopt nutrition concepts faster when tied to familiar foods and playable choices.

Research & Development

The game’s design draws from established nutrition research and field iteration. It is built to be locally adaptable, so the deck can reflect regional foods and recipes while maintaining a consistent nutrition logic.

Inputs

  • Nutrition research references (NIN)
  • Regional food mapping
  • Field playtests with community groups

Artifacts

  • Deck versions
  • Facilitation notes
  • Recipe and food-group sheets

Deployment & Reach

Over 1,000 community members in Chhattisgarh and Uttarakhand have played the game. It has also been played across Kerala, Uttar Pradesh, Odisha, Gujarat, Haryana, Rajasthan, and Madhya Pradesh. Through the Active Citizenship Festival, it has been used in practitioner and campus settings (including Chitkara University, IIM Ahmedabad, and the Institute of Home Economics).

Outcomes & Reach

  • People reached: 1 lakh+ (reported reach through deployments and adoption).
  • Adoption: Adopted by JEEViKA (NRLM) Bihar; pilot with 35,000 SHGs in Gaya district.
  • Supported deployments: Played as part of UNICEF-supported initiatives.
