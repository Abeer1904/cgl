What TACF is

TACF is a modular programme built from games, policy escape rooms, facilitated simulations, and learning modules. It is designed to build shared vocabulary, decision readiness, and civic and institutional capability through structured play.

Experiential learning

Move from awareness to practice. Participants learn by making choices and defending them.

Decision rehearsal

Surface incentives, constraints, and trade-offs before they become real-world failures.

Deployable format

Works across campuses, institutions, districts, and corporate teams. Modular by design.

Who this is for

TACF is designed as a deployable service for institutions working with complexity, people, and real constraints.

Universities and educational institutions

Civic learning, critical thinking, participation, and systems literacy through play-based modules.

  • Orientation and leadership programmes
  • Clubs, societies, and civic engagement
  • Cross-discipline learning formats

Government and public institutions

Preparedness, planning, and citizen-facing engagement through simulations and facilitated labs.

  • District and city-level trainings
  • Programme design and communication
  • Participatory planning and alignment

Corporates and CSR

Leadership development, ethics and trade-off literacy, and decision-making under uncertainty.

  • Strategy offsites and leadership labs
  • ESG, climate, and risk awareness
  • Team alignment through simulations

What happens inside

Pick a bundle, or curate a programme. Modules can be mixed based on theme, audience, and time.

Core

Policy Escape Rooms

Issue-based, time-bound decision environments that force collaboration under constraints.

Core

Civic Games Mela

A curated showcase of playable games across climate, governance, gender, and digital systems.

Optional

Simulation labs

Facilitated scenario runs that surface trade-offs, incentives, and second-order effects.

Optional

Structured debates

Parliamentary-style formats for argument, listening, and democratic reasoning.

Optional

Fake-to-Fact formats

Play-based misinformation and media literacy experiences adapted to local contexts.

Optional

Habit and learning workshops

Short modules to build participation habits and practical civic vocabulary.

Outcomes

TACF is designed to improve decision quality and participation, not just awareness.

Shared vocabulary

Teams and cohorts leave with a common language for complex issues.

Trade-off literacy

Participants learn to articulate costs, consequences, and constraints in decisions.

Systems thinking

People see interdependencies, incentives, and second-order effects.

Action readiness

Learners move from opinion to agency, with clearer pathways for action.

Evidence and feedback

Play creates structured insight you can use for programme design and learning.

Cohort alignment

Institutions get faster alignment across roles without flattening disagreement.

Delivery model

Choose based on time, cohort size, and how deep you want to go.

One-day deployment

A focused selection of modules for rapid learning and engagement.

Ideal for: corporate trainings, campus programmes, short institutional workshops

Two-day flagship TACF

The full festival format with multiple modules and curated pathways.

Ideal for: universities, convenings, institutional capacity building

Travelling series

Multi-site deployments, enabling deeper learning and wider diffusion.

Ideal for: networks, multi-campus circuits, districts, CSR portfolios
