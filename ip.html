Narrative Structure Lab

Translating South Asian ideas into playable systems. We identify narratives and frameworks that can become interactive worlds, translate them into game bibles, and circulate them to the right ecosystem.

Fiction and non-fiction tracks We do not buy IP We build game bibles and dossiers We circulate to studios and agents

What this is

Games are structured stories. This lab takes literary imagination and systems design and converts them into playable architecture. We do not produce games. We build the underlying blueprint, the bible, and the option-ready dossier, and then connect it to studios, agents, and global partners.

Two tracks

Track I

Fiction to global game IP

We focus on South Asian fantasy, speculative fiction, action-adventure, mythic worlds, and political thrillers, and translate them into game bibles that can travel globally.

1. Identify

We sign authors and identify one book or series with strong systemic potential for play.

2. Translate

We convert the narrative into a structured game bible with world logic, characters, progression, and playable premise.

3. Circulate

We prepare an option-ready IP dossier and circulate it to studios, narrative teams, agents, and partners.

What the bible includes

  • World logic and systems
  • Core playable premise and loop
  • Characters, arcs, and moral conflict
  • Progression pathways and scope tiers (indie / AA / AAA)
  • Audience positioning and tonal references

Commercial model

We do not buy IP. We sign you up, translate the work into a game bible, and circulate it. For fiction, the commercial upside is tied to optioning and deal pathways.

Track II

Non-fiction to interactive frameworks

We work with academics, philosophers, research institutions, and think tanks to translate ideas, models, and frameworks into interactive formats that engage wider audiences.

1. Map the framework

Identify variables, incentives, constraints, actors, and tensions inside the idea.

2. Convert into interaction

Design interactive structures such as simulations, game-based learning tools, or public engagement formats.

3. Deploy

Package for classrooms, programmes, MEL, or public-facing knowledge translation.

What we build

  • Simulation structures and sandboxes
  • Game-based learning experiences
  • Interactive research communication formats
  • Tools that support baseline, midline, and endline learning

Commercial model

This is a paid service. We are commissioned to translate theory into structured interaction and deployable learning tools.
